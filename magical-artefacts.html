Magical Artefacts

Explore some of the most famous magical artefacts in the wizarding world.

The Elder Wand
The Elder Wand is one of the three Deathly Hallows. It is the most powerful wand ever made and is said to never fail its master.
The Invisibility Cloak
Another of the Deathly Hallows, the Invisibility Cloak grants the wearer complete invisibility and has been passed down through generations.
The Resurrection Stone
The Resurrection Stone, the third of the Deathly Hallows, is said to bring back the spirits of the deceased, though they are not truly alive.
The Marauder's Map
A magical map created by James Potter, Sirius Black, Remus Lupin, and Peter Pettigrew. It shows the entire Hogwarts castle and its inhabitants.
The Philosopher's Stone
A magical object that can transform any metal into pure gold and create the Elixir of Life, granting immortality to its possessor.
The Time-Turner
A magical time-travel device that allows the user to travel back in time, though with limited use to prevent major paradoxes.
The Sword of Gryffindor
A goblin-made sword that belonged to Godric Gryffindor, it is imbued with magic and can absorb the qualities of substances it comes into contact with.
The Hufflepuff Cup
A golden cup owned by Helga Hufflepuff, one of the Hogwarts founders. It became one of Voldemort's Horcruxes.
The Ravenclaw Diadem
A crown worn by Rowena Ravenclaw, one of the Hogwarts founders. It was also turned into a Horcrux by Voldemort.
The Slytherin Locket
A locket belonging to Salazar Slytherin, one of the Hogwarts founders. It was later turned into a Horcrux by Voldemort.
The Triwizard Cup
A magical cup that serves as the prize in the Triwizard Tournament. It was used by Barty Crouch Jr. to transport Harry Potter to Voldemort.
Fawkes's Tears
Fawkes, Dumbledore's phoenix, can heal people with his tears, which have powerful restorative properties.
The Vanishing Cabinet
A magical object capable of transporting people from one place to another, often used for secretive and clandestine purposes.
The Sorting Hat
The magical hat used at Hogwarts to sort students into one of the four houses: Gryffindor, Slytherin, Ravenclaw, and Hufflepuff.
The Black Family Tapestry
A magical tapestry that traces the genealogical lineage of the Black family, showing members and their relationships.
The Quidditch World Cup Trophy
A prestigious trophy awarded to the winner of the Quidditch World Cup, a symbol of magical sporting excellence.
The Cloak of Invisibility
A rare and powerful magical item that allows the wearer to become invisible. Often associated with the Deathly Hallows.
